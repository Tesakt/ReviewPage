Reviews of stories

Mother of Learning

by nobody103
as Domagoj Kurmaic


Mother of Learning is a fantasy novel that revolves around the concept of a time loop. The story follows Zorian, a teenage mage attending his third year of education at Cyoria's magical academy. Zorian is a driven and irritable young man, consumed by a desire to succeed and break free from the influence of his family. However, when he is killed on the eve of the Cyoria's annual summer festival, he is brought back to the beginning of the month and trapped in a time loop with no clear end or exit.

As Zorian struggles to unravel the mystery of the time loop and find a way out, he must also confront his own flaws and prejudices. Through the repetition of the same events, he learns to see the world and the people around him in a new light. The novel explores themes of growth and self-discovery, as well as the dangers of magic and time travel.

Mother of Learning is an engaging and well-written fantasy novel that sets itself apart from other stories in the genre. The concept of a time loop is fresh and intriguing, and the mystery aspect of the story is well-executed. The main character, Zorian, is complex and relatable, and his growth and development throughout the novel is satisfying to see. The writing is simple and straightforward, with just enough description to bring the world to life without bogging down the narrative.

One of the standout aspects of Mother of Learning is the way it subverts traditional fantasy tropes. Zorian is a protagonist who starts out flawed and grows along the way, rather than the typical chosen one who is destined for greatness. The story also lacks the typical hero's journey and three-act structure that is common in fantasy novels, and instead focuses on the character development and mystery of the time loop.

I recommend Mother of Learning to everyone that loves fantasy and time travel stories alike. It is a unique and captivating novel that explores its central concept in an exciting and thought-provoking way. The characters are well-developed, the writing is effective, and the world-building is immersive. It is a must-read for anyone looking for something fresh and different in the fantasy genre.
